## Course code: MCH2234

## Course Learning Outcome

Students will:

- Be able to explain the unique perspectives that statistical treatment of data brings to the study of chemistry.
- Understand chromatographic separations and solvent extractions.
- Study various advanced separation techniques crucial for both qualitative and quantitative analysis of samples.
- Extend existing electroanalytical methods to new types of samples, and to learn about their applications in the areas of photoelectron chemistry.
- Study the instrument layout and understand the working of various components of instruments.

## Syllabus

- **Evaluation of Analytical Data:** The uses of Statistics - the standard deviation of computed results. Method of least squares, Methods for reporting analytical data,
  Confidence intervals, Introduction to Error Analysis, Instruments Architecture and methods of detection.
- **Separation Techniques:** Super critical fluid chromatography - Principle, instrumentation, and Applications of SCF,
  Super critical extraction chromatography: Principles, advantages and applications, Capillary electrophoresis - Capillary electro-chromatography,
  Solvent extraction - Multiple batch extraction, Counter-current distribution,
  Solid phase extraction - flow injection analysis - reverse osmosis, electro-dialysis, Affinity chromatography,
  Ultra-High-Performance Liquid Chromatography (UHPLC), Pyrolysis gas chromatography; Fast Protein Liquid Chromatography.
- **Electroanalytical Methods:** Different types of electrodes, mercury electrode, solid electrodes, chemically modified electrodes and microelectrodes,
  Voltammetry - pulse voltammetry (normal, differential, square wave, stair case voltammetry), AC voltammetry,
  chronoamperometry-bi-amperometry, Cyclic Voltammetry,
  Stripping Methods (anodic, cathodic, and adsorptive) and applications.
  Introduction to Photo-electrochemistry at semiconductor electrodes, Photoemission from metal electrodes, Electro chemical monitoring of photolytic intermediates, Electrochemiluminescence.
- **Radio-analytical Methods:** Principle of Radio analytical methods - Isotope dilution analysis, Radiometric titrations,
  Radiometric titrations, precipitation and complex formation titrations.
- **Instrumentation:** UV-Visible instrumentation - light sources, detectors, Infra-Red Spectroscopy - principle, instrumentation, Luminescence, Nuclear Magnetic Resonance,
  Mass spectrometer with various detectors - Electron multipliers, Faraday cup, Photomultiplier conversion dynode, Array detectors,
  Atomic absorption spectrometry, Flame photometry - principles and applications.
